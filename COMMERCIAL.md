these allow free noncommercial use, and free security research and

## You Need a Paid Commercial License If You Want To

- Embed MCL in a product or service offered for a fee.
- Deploy MCL internally in a for-profit organization for any purpose beyond the

- Offer MCL (or a service based on it) as a managed or hosted service.
- Redistribute MCL as part of a commercial or OEM offering.
- Make any other use that is not a permitted noncommercial purpose under

## What a Commercial License Typically Includes

A negotiated commercial agreement will generally cover:

1. **Copyright license** — for the agreed commercial scope (term or perpetual;
   named entity; affiliate coverage).
2. **Commercial patent license** — under PCT/IB2026/052737, PCT/IB2026/053253,
   PCT/IB2026/053673, and any patents issuing from them, co-extensive with the
   grant scope.
3. **Scope and restrictions** — defined fields of use; sublicensing limits.
4. **Fees** — one-time, per-deployment, or annual subscription.
5. **Term and termination** — material-breach termination with a cure period;
   survival of confidentiality, IP, and liability provisions.
6. **Warranty and disclaimer** — a limited warranty of title and
   non-infringement, with implied warranties disclaimed.
7. **Limitation of liability** — typically capped at fees paid.
8. **Indemnification** — as negotiated.
9. **Governing law and dispute resolution** — to be agreed. A common neutral
   default for cross-border deals is the law of England and Wales with
   arbitration under the LCIA Rules in London; alternatives are available
   depending on the parties.
10. **Supersession** — the commercial agreement supersedes the
    PolyForm Noncommercial License and the Security Research Grant **solely for
    the licensee's in-scope deployments**; for everyone else, and for the
    licensee's out-of-scope activities, the public licenses continue to apply.
