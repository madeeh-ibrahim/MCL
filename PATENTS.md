## 3. Provisional Rights Notice — 35 U.S.C. § 154(d) (and foreign equivalents)

Pursuant to 35 U.S.C. § 154(d), **upon publication** of a PCT application (and
of any corresponding national application), and after issuance of the
corresponding patent, the patentee may be entitled to obtain a reasonable
royalty from any person who, during the period between publication and issuance,
(a) made, used, offered for sale, sold, or imported the invention as claimed in
the published application, and (b) had **actual notice** of the published
application.

**Timing.** The earliest priority date in this family is 22 March 2026;
international (PCT) publication is expected approximately 18 months thereafter
(around September 2027). The provisional-rights / reasonable-royalty mechanism
described above **does not operate until that publication occurs**; it is dormant
until then. This document is published now so that, once each application
publishes, it serves as a continuing record of **actual notice** from that
publication date forward. Filing (and thus priority) is already secured;
only the notice/royalty mechanism awaits publication. Equivalent
provisional-protection doctrines in other jurisdictions (for example, Article 67
of the European Patent Convention) are also relied upon to the extent available,
on the same publication-dependent basis.
