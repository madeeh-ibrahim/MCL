## 3. Recipient's Undertakings

By signing, the Recipient agrees that:

(a) **Lawful research only.** The Recipient will use the Toolkit solely for
    lawful security research, evaluation, and education, consistent with the
    Grant, including its Acceptable Use and No Misuse section (Grant §8).

(b) **Own / authorized systems only.** The Recipient will run the Toolkit only
    against the Recipient's own copy of MCL, or against systems the Recipient
    owns or is expressly authorized in writing to test. The Recipient will not
    use the Toolkit to access, attack, disrupt, or interfere with any system,
    network, or data of any third party without authorization.

(c) **No public redistribution of the Toolkit files.** The Recipient will not
    republish, re-host, or otherwise distribute the Maintainer's Toolkit files
    for anonymous public download. The Recipient **may** publish the Recipient's
    own findings, analysis, and independently written code, and **must** cite
    the work per `CITATION.cff`.

(d) **Coordinated disclosure.** The Recipient will report any vulnerability or
    significant cryptanalytic finding via the process in `SECURITY.md`.

(e) **No warranty.** The Toolkit is provided "AS IS", without warranty of any
    kind, to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law.

(f) **Sole responsibility.** The Recipient is solely responsible for ensuring
    that the Recipient's use complies with all laws applicable to the Recipient.
    The Maintainer does not endorse, and accepts no responsibility or liability
    for, any unlawful or unauthorized use by the Recipient.

(g) **Revocation.** Access may be revoked if any undertaking in this Agreement is
    breached, or if the Recipient's identity cannot be verified.

## 4. Maintainer's Position

The Maintainer provides the Toolkit for the stated research purpose, records this
